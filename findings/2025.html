Pakistan Report Card 2025

Summary of grades and key findings across all 10 Global Matrix indicators.

Overall Physical Activity D

Benchmark

Percentage of children and adolescents meeting WHO’s recommendation of at least 60 minutes of moderate-to-vigorous physical activity daily.

Key Findings

  • Only X% of Pakistani youth meet physical activity guidelines.
  • Boys are generally more active than girls.
Organized Sport Participation C-

Benchmark

Proportion of children and adolescents participating in organized sports at least once a week.

Key Findings

  • Participation is higher among urban boys compared to rural girls.
  • Limited infrastructure and access remain barriers.
Active Play C

Benchmark

Proportion of children engaging in unstructured active play several times per week.

Key Findings

  • Traditional games are common but declining in urban settings.
Active Transportation B

Benchmark

Proportion of children using active means (walking, cycling) to travel to and from school.

Key Findings

  • Many children walk to school, especially in rural areas.
Sedentary Behaviours C+

Benchmark

Proportion of children spending less than 2 hours daily on recreational screen time.

Key Findings

  • Screen time is increasing rapidly in urban populations.
Physical Fitness C

Benchmark

Average physical fitness levels (cardiorespiratory, muscular strength) relative to international norms.

Key Findings

  • Limited national data; school-level studies suggest moderate fitness levels.
Family and Peers C-

Benchmark

Proportion of parents and peers providing support for physical activity (encouragement, participation, transportation).

Key Findings

  • Parental support varies by socioeconomic status.
School B-

Benchmark

Proportion of schools offering regular physical education classes and activity opportunities.

Key Findings

  • PE classes exist in policy but are not consistently implemented.
Community and Environment C

Benchmark

Availability and accessibility of community-level facilities supporting physical activity.

Key Findings

  • Limited safe and inclusive public spaces for play.
Government C+

Benchmark

Evidence of leadership, policies, and investments supporting physical activity for children and youth.

Key Findings

  • Recent national policies acknowledge physical activity, but implementation remains limited.
